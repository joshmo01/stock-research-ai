Fundamental analysis involves evaluating a company's intrinsic value by examining related economic and financial factors. Key metrics include:

1. Valuation Metrics:
- Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
- Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
- Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio
- Enterprise Value/EBITDA

2. Financial Health:
- Current Ratio
- Debt-to-Equity Ratio
- Return on Equity (ROE)
- Return on Assets (ROA)

3. Growth Metrics:
- Revenue Growth
- Earnings Growth
- Free Cash Flow Growth
- Dividend Growth

4. Profitability:
- Gross Margin
- Operating Margin
- Net Profit Margin
- EBITDA Margin

Consider industry averages and historical trends when analyzing these metrics. Look for:
- Consistent growth in key metrics
- Strong competitive advantages
- Healthy balance sheet
- Sustainable business model
- Quality management team
- Economic moat
